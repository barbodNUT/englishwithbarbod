Part 2: The Rainy Day (Intermediate)

Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verbs in brackets.

Yesterday afternoon, while I was waiting (wait) for the bus, it started (start) to rain. I didn't have (not/have) an umbrella. Luckily, I saw (see) my friend Barbod across the street. He was walking (walk) towards his car. Today, the weather is much better. At this very moment, the birds are singing (sing) and people are enjoying (enjoy) the park. I always carry (always/carry) an umbrella now because I hate (hate) getting wet!
Explanation:
  • Past Continuous vs Simple: Use was waiting and was walking for longer background actions, and started and saw for short completed events that happened during those actions.
  • Present Continuous: Are singing/enjoying are used for things happening right now ("at this very moment").
  • Stative Verbs: Verbs like hate are almost never used in the continuous (-ing) form.
